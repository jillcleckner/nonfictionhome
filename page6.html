They came and built grand homes
On this virgin earth with bare hands.
Raised generations of family there.
Over a hundred years have since past.
Blight.

Once a source of great pride
Now reduced to condemned homes
In a quickly spreading, desolate landscape
Of gaping holes where life once thrived.
Creeping in every direction.
Claiming its victims one by one.
Blight.

Signs of disorder dominate the slums.
Boarded up windows
Like eyelids permanently shut
So as not to see the dilapidation
Left carelessly behind.
No one wants to see.
Blight.

Single homes left standing in isolation.
Like magnets to crime and gangs.
Drugs, weapons and trash sleep in the
Overgrown empty lots
Void of flowers.
Blight.

Once neatly manicured yards
Filled with laughing children,
Men pushing lawn mowers,
Women talking over fences.
Who let this beauty die?
The sad cycle of urban decay.
Blight.
